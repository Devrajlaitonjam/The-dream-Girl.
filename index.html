Part One

The First Vision

I saw her for the very first time in the quiet theatre of my dreams, somewhere between the 2nd and 4th standard — a time when the world still felt vast and unknowable (2012–2014). She appeared like a gentle ray of dawn breaking through the dark, arriving not to haunt but to bless. She came bearing the most precious gift: the promise that I had already triumphed — that somewhere in the future, the exam had been cleared, the battle had been won.

And with that promise came another, even more precious: she would be there for me, steadfast as a lighthouse, expecting me until the very last breath of her life. In that dream, the feeling was so real, so vivid, that even waking could not erase it. She followed me like a guardian angel — not in a way that frightened, but in a way that warmed the coldest corners of my heart.

"Though her face was always a soft blur, like morning mist over still water, her presence was unmistakable."
Part Two

A Guardian Through the Darkest Hours

Whenever the world grew heavy on my shoulders — whenever doubt crept in like a thief in the night and I was on the very edge of surrendering — she would come. Just once or twice a year, like a rare and precious comet crossing the sky, she would appear in my dreams and breathe courage back into my weary soul. She was the most beautiful woman I had ever seen: like a fairy princess woven from moonlight and stardust, her skin as soft as silk and as pure as fresh-fallen snow.

Her voice, though I cannot recall its exact sound, carried the warmth of a thousand sunsets. She reminded me, again and again, that I could not afford to lose my way — that my destination was worth every stumble, every sleepless night, every silent tear.

"She was not merely a dream; she was my compass."

Part Three

The Reunion Dream

Then, after years of these fleeting visits, she returned to me in the most glorious dream of all — on the 16th of May, 2026, between 2:45 and 3:31 in the afternoon. And this time, the dream did not end in longing. This time, we had won.

There I was, at the High School Reunion of the 2017–2020 batch, walking into that room not as the boy who had once trembled with uncertainty, but as a man who had carved his success with his own hands. My dear friends — Romesh, Arjun, and Amir Hussain — were there too, each glowing with their own triumphs. Even Amir's spectacles had grown thicker with the wisdom of the years, a quiet, endearing reminder of time's gentle passage.

Part Four

Her Presence at My Side

And she was there. She was actually there. She clung to my right arm as though I were the very anchor of her world, and I — lost completely in the warmth of her nearness — would not have let go for anything. While I laughed and caught up with old friends, swapping stories from years gone by, she remained there, a quiet warmth against my arm, her presence more eloquent than any words.

When three haughty girls from our class attempted their familiar mockery, she was my shield without even raising her voice — her mere presence silencing the cruelty with a dignity that left no room for unkindness.

Part Five

A Dinner Beneath the Stars

At dinner, I found myself telling her how to find True North using the Great Bear constellation — how to trace the line from the two pointer stars of Ursa Major to the steadfast North Star, faithful and unmoving in its place in the heavens. She listened with those beautiful eyes — those impossibly beautiful eyes — drinking in every word not merely as knowledge, but as poetry.

Her gaze was full of a love so pure it made the stars themselves feel close. We shared quiet smiles at the knowing giggles of our friends, and then we slipped away to the room that felt like the entire universe, just the two of us, where time stopped, and the world outside ceased to matter entirely.

She is tall, graceful, and luminous — a figure that seems to belong both to dreams
and to the very best version of waking life. Her eyes hold entire galaxies.
Her smile could end a thousand sleepless nights.


Please, God — do not take this memory from me.
Let me carry it for the rest of my days, as a lantern in the dark.
She is my dream, my direction, my North Star.


I do not know when she will arrive in my waking life,
but I know this with every fibre of my being:
when she does, I will make her my life.
I will make her my everything.


And until that blessed day, I ask for just one mercy —
let me see her every night when I close my eyes.
Let every dream I dream be filled with her presence,
for even in sleep, she is my greatest joy,
my most beautiful reason to shut the world away
and surrender peacefully to the night.
